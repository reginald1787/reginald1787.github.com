Bayesian methods in ranking

by Li

Posted on August 24, 2013 at 9:00 PM

Many websites contain a ranking system, like Quora, Reddit, Zhihu, for a post/question, there might be multiple responses/answers, and for each response/answer, users could have two options: upvote or downvote. 

Generally the number of upvote and down vote should decide the ranking of the response/answer. (There may be some extra factors needed to consider for ranking though.)

In this case, a Bayesian approach could be useful. Let us assume that the ranking is purely decided by the number of upvotes, while the number of upvotes is a Binomial distribution with n total users and voting probability p.

Now the question is: how to decide p appropriately? The Bayesian answer would be to calculate the conjugate prior probability and then update with data.

Before going further, let us take a look at the conjugate prior distribution diagram:

From this we know that the conjugate prior probability of p follows Beta distribution, Beta(a,b), which pdf is proportional to the p^(a-1) and (1-p)^(b-1).

And to update p with data we calculate the posterior probability:  p|data ~ Beta(a+upvotes, b+downvotes).

This simple method could basically achieve:

1. more upvotes would high-rank the answer

2. more downvotes would low-rank the answer  

3. the ranking would gradually change rather than suddenly jump to top or bottom.
